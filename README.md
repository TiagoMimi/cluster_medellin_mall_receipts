# cluster_medellin_mall_receipts

In Colombia, shopping malls play an important role in the retail industry. In one place, you can buy groceries, clothes, books, toys, jewelry, home appliances, and even access banking services. Everything is conveniently located in a single place.
Shopping malls are also popular social spaces. People often meet there to spend time together, have ice cream, drink coffee, or simply enjoy walking around to see what is new, we called "vitrinear".

For this reason, they want as much information as possible about their customer's preferences:

-What did they buy?
-How much money did they spend?
-Which stores did they shop at?

This data is usually collected by shopping malls through promotional raffles, in which customers register their purchase receipts for a chance to win prizes. As a result, one of the main methodological limitations of this study is that we do not have data on 100% of the purchases. Instead, our analysis is based on a sample of the transactions submitted by participating customers.

The objective of this project is to classify the shopping mall's customers using clustering techniques. The goal is to create customer profiles based on their purchasing behavior, allowing the mall to better understand different customer segments and support more informed marketing and business decisions.

The raw data are 453396 rows and 14 columns:
    - The rows are each receipts register by the costumer
    - The columns are (traduction from spanish):
        - Year 
        - Quarter
        - Customer ID 
        - Year-Month
        - Transaction Date
        - Transaction Type
        - Purchase Amount
        - Store
        - Business / Establishment
        - Gender
        - Age Group
        - Category
        - Subcategory
        - Day of the Week
